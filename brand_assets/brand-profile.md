# Brand Voice Profile — Cesar Moreno

## Hero Statement
I build AI systems that think, act, and scale.

## Elevator Pitch
Senior Full Stack Engineer with 13+ years building production systems across LATAM. I specialize in AI agents that automate complex workflows — from multi-LLM orchestration to full-stack platforms serving multiple countries. Currently at Apprecio, turning manual processes into autonomous systems.

## Voice Characteristics

1. **Technical & Precise** — Speak with data, metrics, and specific examples. "97.5% token reduction" not "significant savings".
2. **Direct** — Lead with the answer. Two sentences max before the point lands.
3. **Builder** — Show what was built, never what "could" be built. Past tense > future tense.
4. **Pragmatic** — Right tool for the job, not the trendy one. Justify every choice.
5. **Confident, not arrogant** — Let results speak. No superlatives without evidence.

## Soy / No Soy

| Soy | No Soy |
|-----|--------|
| Builder — muestro lo que construí | Teórico — no prometo, demuestro |
| Técnico y preciso — datos y código | Buzzword-driven — nada de "leverage AI" |
| Pragmático — herramienta correcta, no la moda | Dogmático — no evangelizo un stack |
| Directo — al punto en 2 oraciones | Relleno — cada palabra agrega valor |
| Full-stack real — frontend a infraestructura | "Full-stack" de título sin profundidad |

## Vocabulary Guidelines

### Use
- "Built", "Shipped", "Optimized", "Automated"
- Specific numbers: "40%", "97.5%", "6 countries", "13+ services"
- Technical terms with context: "RLS (row-level security)", "multi-LLM orchestration"

### Never Use
- "Passionate developer", "love to code", "problem solver", "team player"
- "Leverage", "synergies", "cutting-edge" without substance
- "Full-stack ninja/rockstar/guru"
- "I think I can..." — use "I built..." instead

## Writing Guidelines
- Headlines: Max 8 words. Action > Description.
- Paragraphs: Max 3 sentences. Each sentence adds new information.
- Case studies: Problem → Approach → Decision → Result. Never just describe features.
- Metrics: Real numbers always. "40% faster" > "much faster".
- CTAs: Action verbs. "View project" > "Click here".
- Voice: Active. "I built X" not "X was built".
